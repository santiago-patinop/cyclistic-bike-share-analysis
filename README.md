# Data Analysis Project: "Cyclistic" Case Study

**General Description**
This repository contains the analysis for the "Cyclistic" case study, developed as the capstone project for the Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate. The main objective is to understand the differences in bicycle usage between casual riders and annual members. With a strong focus on business intelligence, this project seeks to optimize current systems and design a marketing strategy with a clear achievement orientation: converting casual users into profitable annual members.

---

## 1. Data Origin and Preparation

The information processing integrates industrial engineering principles with structured data analysis to ensure the model's viability.

* **Source:** Real bicycle trip records provided by Motivate International Inc., the operator of the Divvy system in Chicago.
* **Period:** 12 months of historical data, spanning from June 2025 to May 2026.
* **Extraction and Consolidation:** A process was automated using the Kaggle API to download 12 monthly CSV files, which were concatenated into a master DataFrame consisting of 5,848,703 records.

### Transformation and Cleaning

To unlock temporal dimensions and ensure data integrity for decision-making, the following transformations were executed:

* The text columns `started_at` and `ended_at` were converted to *datetime* objects.
* The `ride_length_mins` variable was generated by subtracting the start time from the end time to calculate the total duration of each trip in minutes.
* Granular time dimensions were extracted: day of the week (`day_name`), month of the year (`month_name`), and hour of the day (`hour_of_day`).
* A strict filter was applied to remove anomalies and input errors, discarding 29 records with durations less than or equal to 0 minutes.
* The result is a dataset of 5,848,674 clean records ready for analysis.

## 2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

As an analyst, it is vital that visualizations and findings are easy to understand for clients or stakeholders, allowing them to adjust their portfolios and operations based on evidence. Utilizing assertive communication, the critical findings are highlighted below:

### Average Ride Duration by User Type

* Casual riders average significantly longer trips, at 22.26 minutes.
* Annual members record an average duration of almost half that, at 12.47 minutes.

> **Business Interpretation:** This strong contrast indicates that casual users primarily use the service for leisure and recreation. Meanwhile, annual members rely on the bicycles for short, frequent, and routine commutes to specific destinations.

### Duration and Volume Patterns by Day of the Week

* **Casual User Behavior:** They present highly variable ride durations throughout the week (between 18.6 and 25.7 minutes) and experience an obvious peak on weekends, reaching 24.9 minutes on Saturdays and 25.7 minutes on Sundays.
* **Annual Member Behavior:** They exhibit a highly stable pattern, maintaining a narrow duration range between 12.0 and 13.7 minutes regardless of the day of the week. This reaffirms their behavior centered on regular point-to-point commuting.
